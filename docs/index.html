What Makes a Beer Popular?

What truly defines the popularity of a beer? Is it the irresistible aroma that wafts from the glass? The vibrant clarity of its appearance? Or is it the bold, complex taste that lingers on the palate? To answer these questions, we dove headfirst into the RateBeer dataset—home to over 50,000 users, nearly 400,000 beers, and millions of reviews. These reviews aren't just opinions; they're carefully structured evaluations across several key attributes:

  • Aroma: The beer's smell, scored out of 10.
  • Appearance: Its color, clarity, head, and visual carbonation, scored out of 5.
  • Taste: The flavor, including bitterness, palate, and finish, scored out of 10.
  • Mouthfeel: The beer’s body, carbonation, and astringency, scored out of 5. (Called Palate in the dataset)
  • Overall: A holistic score that captures the personal experience and the beer’s characteristics, scored out of 20.

Together, these metrics form a total score out of 50, which is then scaled to a score out of 5. This comprehensive approach allows us to see how each aspect contributes to a beer’s success—or lack thereof. Unsurprisingly, taste and aroma often steal the spotlight when it comes to high ratings. But there’s more to the story, as we discovered.

The Connections: What Attributes Shape Popularity?

To uncover the recipe for beer success, we examined the relationships between these attributes. The correlation matrix below paints a fascinating picture. Taste and aroma show a strong connection to the overall score, which makes sense—after all, these are the two most sensory aspects of beer. Appearance and mouthfeel, while less dominant, still play meaningful supporting roles. And what about alcohol content (ABV)? It has an intriguing, if less potent, influence on overall ratings.

Here’s what we found:

  • Taste and Aroma: With correlations of 0.65 and 0.77 respectively, these attributes are the MVPs of beer ratings. If the taste or aroma falls flat, the overall score is unlikely to shine.
  • Appearance and Mouthfeel: While less influential, these attributes (0.5 and 0.66 correlations, respectively) still matter. A beer that looks and feels great can elevate the experience.
  • Alcohol Content (ABV): At 0.37, the correlation with overall ratings is noticeable but not overwhelming. This tells us that while ABV adds to the experience, it’s not the star of the show.

The takeaway? A great beer is more than the sum of its parts. Taste and aroma lay the foundation, but appearance, mouthfeel, and even alcohol content provide the finishing touches. This balance is what creates a truly memorable beer.
